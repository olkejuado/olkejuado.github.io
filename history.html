A Center for Excellence

Since its founding in 1958, Olkejuado High School has been committed to excellence in equiping students with skills, knowledge and attitudes to diligently transform our society

The History of OHS

The genesis of Olkejuado High School could perhaps be dated to the year 1958. The school was started as an intermediate primary school from standard 4 to 8 and was then called Maasai Government School (MGS).

Later on in 1965, the school was elevated to a secondary school then known as Olkejuado secondary school (OSS). The first from one class had only one girl, Grace Nemayian, admission number 042 (now Mrs. Konchellah).

By 1968, the school had an intake of two more classes but since there was no space, one class was taken to Narok secondary school and the other to Kiserian Seminary. In the same year, the United States through USAID put up new classrooms and dormitories, then other facilities in 1969. The Narok and the Kiserian classes then joined the school to form a three streamed school

In 1977, a Harambee stream was started. The government took over the Harambee stream in May 1982; and the school was approved to be a four-streamed school of 640 students.

The school developed into a full-fledged high school in 1979, when an Advanced Level Science class was started. It was then renamed to Olkejuado High School (OHS).

All Boys School

In 1986, the 8-4-4 education system was introduced in the country, and Olkejuado High School became a boys only school. Two years later, in 1988, a second Advanced Level Arts class was put in place on Harambee basis. A year later, the last form six class sat for their A-Level examinations. Admissions for girls was phased out in 1992.

OHS was a provincial school for many years with intakes from the wider Rift Valley province and beyond. Currently, it is a county school with an enrollment of 665 students. The teaching staff stands at 49 (B.O.M. teachers inclusive).
